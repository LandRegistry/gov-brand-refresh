GOV.UK Brand Refresh at HM Land Registry

GOV.UK underwent a brand refresh which went live on 25th June.

The refreshed branding consists of:

  • a new header which is now fully blue, containing only the logo (Crown and GOVUK text) that links to the homepage
  • a footer with a light blue background and small crown logo

There will also be additional changes required such as moving the service name from the header into the navigation component.

All services we host on a GDS domain needed to be updated by us, to the new branding.

These can be identified by:

  • if the url for the webpage is in the style of gov.uk/myservice. In this instance the branding changes updated automatically
  • if the url ends with .service.gov.uk. In this instance service teams needed to update and apply the new branding styles

After compiling a list of services with the help of Product and Design as well as carrying out an Audit of nearly all our services, products and webpages there were only 3 services on a GDS domain:

  • Search for land and property information
  • Search for local land charges
  • Use land a property data

The audit also revealed where we had services using the GOV.UK branding incorrectly as they weren’t on GDS domains. These services changed their branding to HMLR. The list of services including final branding status gives the full list.

There were however 2 exceptions where we reached out to GDS and requested to keep GOV.UK branding:

  • Sign your mortgage deed. This service passed its service assessment so is entitled to be on a GDS domain.
  • Citizens account (the sign in and create account pages within Search for Land and Property Information). Moving these pages to HMLR branding would have created an inconsistent user experience.

The advice from GDS was vague and open to interpretation as to keeping GOV branding. 19th June I sent a final email to say we would be adopting the branding and to get in touch if they had any concerns. This was the last correspondence.
